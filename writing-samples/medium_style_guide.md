# Medium Writing Style Guide

Compiled from freeCodeCamp's style guide for Medium, Medium Staff tips, and analysis of top Medium writers.

## Headlines
- Avoid clickbait and listicle formats
- Focus on truth and clarity with matter-of-fact language
- Don't label multi-part series as "Part 1" — it discourages readers
- Keep headlines concise to prevent truncation in feeds
- Titles should be relevant to the reader — if clickbait, they either don't click or quickly bounce

## Paragraph Structure
- Keep paragraphs short: 2-4 lines is the sweet spot
- Mobile users scroll fast and skim — short paragraphs are essential
- Break long paragraphs into shorter units
- One idea per paragraph

## Sentence Structure
- Eliminate semicolons; use periods instead
- Break long sentences into shorter ones
- Target grade 6 readability even for technical content
- Use the Hemingway App to detect passive voice, unnecessary adverbs, and complex vocabulary
- Prioritize accessibility for non-native English speakers

## Tone and Voice
- Write in conversational language using contractions
- Address readers as "you" rather than "we" or "us"
- Maintain consistent verb tense throughout
- Authenticity is your most valuable asset — AI can't infuse genuine voice and emotional depth
- Stories need purpose: connect, teach, or inspire

## Formatting
- Use subheadings (H2s and H3s) to break articles into digestible sections
- Bold key takeaways to create visual anchors
- Use italics to emphasize nuance
- Use bullets when listing steps or ideas
- Use bold OR italic for emphasis — never both together
- Never bold or italicize hyperlinks
- Avoid drop caps

## Punctuation
- Limit exclamation points to genuine exclamations
- Place commas and periods inside quotation marks
- Avoid spaces before colons
- Use English terms instead of Latin abbreviations ("for example" not "e.g.")
- Apply the Oxford Comma

## Links
- Integrate links naturally into sentences
- Only hyperlink a few words rather than full URLs
- Create preview cards by placing vital links on separate lines

## Pull Quotes
- Reserve pull quotes exclusively for multi-line direct quotations from other sources
- Never use them for your own content or self-promotion

## Images
- Always include a high-resolution image under the headline
- Use column width for most images
- Use full-bleed width for charts or data-heavy content
- Properly attribute images using "Image credit" linked to the original source

## Article Length
- Most successful beginner articles: 600-1,200 words
- A concise 800-word tutorial can outperform a 2,000-word thinkpiece if well-focused
- Quality and focus over length

## Acronyms
- Only use acronyms more widely recognized than their full forms
- Define unfamiliar acronyms on first use: "term (ACRONYM)"
- Avoid acronym definitions in opening paragraphs

## Final Tips
- Sleep on it, then proofread before publishing
- Have others review your work
- Don't open with "published on my blog" — place platform notes at the end
- Medium detects and deprioritizes purely AI-generated content
- Writers who focus on 1-2 core topics build loyal readers faster
